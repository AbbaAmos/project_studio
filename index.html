If you reside in New York, data shows that you are likely to be a victim of assualt in the 3rd degree or your chances of being arrested by the police is higher based on color, according to NYPD arrest records in 2022.

With over 30,000 assualt related arrests, it constitutes the bulk of the total arrests carried out by the NYPD when compared with other crimes. While the number of cops in each neighborhood is difficult to ascertain since there is no public data available, the total number of arrests were higher in Brooklyn at 50, 961 compared to other boros in New York.

Over 26 percent of all arrests in New York were made in Brooklyn. 14th Precinct, with over 6,000 arrests made the most arrests over of the 77 precincts in New York.This is followed by 44th, 40th and 75th precincts

The number of black arrests were higher in all five boros in New York compared with other ethnic races. In Brooklyn, over 59 per cent of the total number of arrests by the NYPD were blacks, followed by Whites at 11 per cent.

For example, Staten Island with a majority White population of 57 per cent and Black population of 9 per cent, the total number of arrests by race indicates that 47 per cent were blacks at 3,462 arrests while 28 per cent were Whites.
